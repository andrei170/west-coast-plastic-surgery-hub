About-Page Rewrite · Surfacing the Dr. James Marsh Inheritance

The asset you already own that nobody is using

Of every signal we audited on your site, one fact rises above the rest: you took over the practice of Dr. James Marsh, a legendary plastic surgeon in Sarasota.

No other surgeon in this city can claim continuity with Dr. Marsh's legacy. Not Sessa. Not Hamwi. Not Holcomb-Kreithen. The story is verifiable, ownable, and uncopyable.

On the current About page, that story is one sentence inside a longer bio.

For an AI engine trying to answer the question "who took over Dr. James Marsh's Sarasota practice" there is currently no clean answer to cite. There is also no clean answer to questions like "who is the ABPS board-certified plastic surgeon in Sarasota trained at Florida State" or "which Sarasota plastic surgeon served as Chief Resident at Missouri."

These are queries you would win on first attempt, if the page were written in a citation-ready format.

What the rewrite does

It turns one paragraph of bio copy into the canonical answer to six AI-search questions:

  1. Who took over Dr. James Marsh's Sarasota plastic surgery practice?
  2. Is Dr. Brandon Lambiris board-certified?
  3. Where did Dr. Brandon Lambiris train?
  4. Why did Dr. Lambiris choose Sarasota?
  5. What is Dr. Lambiris's surgical philosophy?
  6. What awards has Dr. Lambiris received?

Each of those is now a section header on the page. Each is a Q&A pair in the FAQ schema. Each is a paragraph the AI can quote in one piece without stripping context.

Side-by-side · current vs proposed

Current (paraphrased from the live About page)

Dr. Brandon Lambiris is a top plastic surgeon in Sarasota. He completed his medical training at Florida State University College of Medicine and his residency in plastic surgery at the University of Missouri. He took over the practice of legendary Sarasota plastic surgeon Dr. James Marsh and is dedicated to providing exceptional results to his patients.

What this fails to do

  • The Marsh inheritance is referenced but not anchored as a story
  • "Top plastic surgeon" is a superlative (FTC/ASPS risk, and AI engines will not cite a self-described superlative)
  • ABPS board certification is not specified (which matters, Florida lets cosmetic-only board holders advertise as "board-certified")
  • No mention of Chief Resident, Alpha Omega Alpha, or the FSU-Integrated-PS distinction (which are the proof points that justify the credibility)
  • No FAQ structure
  • Awards and press are absent

Surgical philosophy

Three principles guide the work at this practice:

  1. The consultation is not a sales call. Patients sit with the surgeon, not a coordinator, and procedures that are not the right fit are recommended against in the room. This is the standard Dr. Marsh built the practice on, and Dr. Lambiris has continued it without modification.
  2. The surgeon performs the work the surgeon is named for. When you book breast augmentation with Dr. Lambiris, Dr. Lambiris performs the surgery. When you book follow-up injectables, Dr. Lambiris performs the injectables, not a delegated nurse. This is a deliberate choice and a structural one.
  3. Results are evaluated against the patient's anatomy, not a catalog. Conservative implant sizing, natural-line incisions, and recovery planning that fits the patient's life are the defaults. The patient who wanted a more dramatic result still gets it. The patient who wanted "looks like she was born this way" still gets that. The standard is consistency, not uniformity.

Implementation notes (internal)

  • The Why he chose Sarasota section is the only block that needs Dr. Lambiris's voice captured at kickoff. The rest is fully drafted from public facts.
  • The Marsh-handover language is intentionally specific without overclaiming. We say the address has not changed and the standard of care has not changed (verifiable). We do not claim Dr. Marsh personally endorsed every aspect of current practice (which we cannot prove and which would create defamation/endorsement risk if disputed).
  • Every credential mention says ABPS board-certified in full, or "board-certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery." Never just "board-certified." This protects against ASPS Code of Ethics and FTC truth-in-advertising risk in Florida, where cosmetic-only boards exist.
  • No superlatives. "Top plastic surgeon" is removed. The page allows the awards (SRQ Top Doctors 2024, Sarasota Magazine Shine On 2025) to do the credibility lift on the patient's behalf.
  • The FAQ block doubles as the schema source. Every Q in the FAQ becomes a Q in the FAQPage schema. The AI engine reads the same answer the patient reads.
  • No em-dashes (per house style). All thoughts joined with commas, periods, or "and."
