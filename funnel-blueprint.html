Financing Trust Block + Schema Sample

Part 1 · Financing trust block

What the audit found

The current homepage does not name a single financing partner above the fold or in the hero. The nav contains a generic "Financing" link. No CareCredit logo. No PatientFi. No Cherry. No Alphaeon. No Prosper Healthcare Lending. No payment calculator. No "from $X/month" anchor next to procedure pricing.

For a practice whose primary procedures price between $6,000 and $17,000, the absence of explicit financing reassurance above the fold is a measurable conversion leak.

Why this matters more for cosmetic surgery than for most verticals

Three documented patterns in cosmetic surgery patient behaviour:

  1. The price-shock moment. A patient lands on a procedure page. They scan to find the price. They see "$8,500 to $12,000." If the next thing their eye finds is not "as low as $189/month with CareCredit," the next thing their eye finds is the back button.
  2. The pre-consult drop-off. Even patients who fill out the consultation form often cancel before the consult, because they have spent the intervening days convinced the procedure is unaffordable. Visible financing before the form converts the same patient at the same price.
  3. The trust-partner effect. CareCredit and PatientFi are names patients already recognise from their dentist, their dermatologist, their veterinarian. Seeing those logos on a plastic surgery site is a soft credentialing signal: "this is a real practice operating inside the normal medical-financing ecosystem."

Copy library for the financing section

Three sentence-length variants the practice can use in different contexts (hero, procedure pages, consultation form, post-consult email). All written to be ASPS Code of Ethics safe and FTC truth-in-advertising safe.

Variant 1 (homepage hero)

Procedures available with financing from CareCredit, PatientFi, Cherry, and Alphaeon Credit. Payment plans from as low as $189/month with approved credit.

Variant 2 (procedure page header)

Most patients finance their procedure. We work with CareCredit, PatientFi, Cherry, and Alphaeon Credit so you can build a payment plan that fits your monthly budget. Apply in minutes, get a decision the same day.

Variant 3 (post-consult follow-up email)

Worth knowing as you decide: the procedures we discussed today are eligible for financing through CareCredit, PatientFi, Cherry, or Alphaeon Credit. Most patients in your situation choose a 24- or 36-month plan, with monthly payments typically in the $250 to $500 range. Happy to walk you through specifics if helpful.

Why these four partners specifically

The four financing partners listed are the four most patient-recognised in U.S. medical aesthetic financing:

  • CareCredit (Synchrony Bank), most widely recognised name in U.S. medical financing across dentistry, dermatology, veterinary, vision. Brand-trust transfer is the value.
  • PatientFi, specifically built for cosmetic surgery, broader credit-tier acceptance than CareCredit, higher procedure-cost ceilings. Strong on the $15K+ procedure tier.
  • Cherry, youngest patient demographic, fastest approval (~60 seconds), no hard credit check. Strong for the under-35 patient segment.
  • Alphaeon Credit (Comenity), specifically positioned for premium aesthetics, longer terms (up to 60 months), promotional 0% APR offers. Strong for $20K+ multi-procedure cases.

This combination covers the full credit-tier and procedure-cost matrix.

Legal and compliance flags

  • Florida BME + FDA + FTC + Truth in Lending Act (TILA): Any "from $X/month" claim requires a representative APR disclosure or a "subject to credit approval" footnote. Both are present in the mockup.
  • CareCredit / PatientFi / Cherry / Alphaeon marketing guidelines: Each partner restricts how their logo may be used. Logo files and use-permission documents are pulled from each partner's provider portal during deployment. No partner logo is used without confirmed active enrolment.
  • Pricing claims: "Procedures from $X" claims must reflect actual practice pricing. The placeholder pricing in this mockup is for layout reference only. Real numbers come from the practice at kickoff.

Part 2 · Deployable schema markup sample

What this is

This is real, deployable JSON-LD structured data for one page on your site: the Breast Augmentation in Sarasota page.

Drop the code block at the bottom into the <head> of that page (or load it via tag manager) and Google's AI gains a machine-readable map of: who Dr. Lambiris is, what credentials he holds, what procedure the page is about, what the consultation process looks like, what the FAQ answers are, and where the practice is physically located.

This is not theoretical. This is the work, ready to ship for one page, as proof.

What the schema declares to Google's AI

Schema TypeWhat It Tells Google
MedicalBusinessThe practice is a medical practice at this address, with these hours, this phone, this rating
PhysicianDr. Lambiris is a board-certified physician with these qualifications, training, and society memberships
MedicalProcedureBreast augmentation is a service this practice provides, here is its description and what it costs
FAQPageEach FAQ on the page is a Q&A pair the AI can quote directly in its answer
BreadcrumbListThe page sits inside the /breast/ procedure category
AggregateRatingThe practice holds a 5.0 rating across this many reviews

What needs verification at kickoff

The schema is structurally complete and valid. The seven [VERIFY_*] placeholders are values that must be confirmed against the live page or the Google Business Profile before deployment:

  1. Logo URL on the site
  2. Exterior practice photo URL
  3. Lat/lon coordinates (pulled from the Google Business Profile)
  4. Dr. Lambiris photo URL
  5. Google Business Profile review count (the real number, not the footer-aggregated 248)
  6. Breast-aug FAQ answer text (copy/paste from the existing page so the AI quotes the exact published copy)
  7. Breast-aug price range and recovery timeline as already published on the page

No fabricated numbers. No invented review counts. No made-up qualifications.

Validation

Before deployment, the code is run through:

  • Google's Rich Results Test (validates structural correctness)
  • Schema.org Validator (validates the schema vocabulary)
  • Manual review against the live page content (validates the values match what is actually published)
