Roadmap

90-Day Roadmap · From 0/7 AEO Citations to a Defensible Citation Moat

What this engagement is

A focused, time-boxed 90-day infrastructure project to make West Coast Plastic Surgery's existing credentials machine-readable to Google's AI, with the Dr. James Marsh inheritance story positioned as the practice's ownable citation moat.

What this engagement is NOT (scope discipline)

This 90 days is explicitly not a paid-media engagement. We are not running ads, building creative, or managing spend on Meta, Google, or TikTok in this window.

This 90 days is explicitly not a social-content engagement. We are not posting Reels, ghostwriting captions, or running an influencer strategy.

Both are valid follow-on engagements. Both are deferred until the AEO foundation is in place, because spending on either before the foundation is built is paying to drive traffic to a site that AI engines, Google's Knowledge Panel, and patient-facing trust signals do not yet support.

What success looks like at day 90

Honest measurement criteria, set in advance:

  1. AEO eligibility lift on the original 7 queries. Baseline: 0 of 7 AI citations. Target: at least 4 of 7 queries where West Coast Plastic Surgery is visible as one of the named sources in Google's AI answer, even if not the top-cited.
  2. GBP rich-result indexing. Star rating + review count appears in standard SERP for branded searches. Baseline: not present. Target: present.
  3. Schema validation. All 6 procedure pages + homepage + About page pass Google Rich Results Test with zero errors. Baseline: not present. Target: 100% pass.
  4. RealSelf review velocity. Baseline: 8 reviews. Target: 25+ reviews.
  5. Procedure-page rank lift. Baseline: page 1 organic on 5 of 7 queries, 0 page-1 on best-of queries. Target: same procedure pages, page-1 rank stable, plus newly written editorial articles ranking page-1 on the 4 educational queries they were written for.

We will commit to those criteria in the engagement contract. We will not commit to a specific revenue number or a specific number of new consultations, because those are downstream of variables (ad spend, seasonality, surgeon capacity) the SEO/AEO work does not control on its own.

What happens after day 90

Three roads, choose at the 90-day review:

  1. Continue with the same scope at a maintenance level (monthly retainer, ongoing editorial + review velocity + GBP). Recommended if Phase 3 measurement shows momentum but consultation volume is still capacity-constrained.
  2. Layer in paid ads + funnel build. Now appropriate because the destination site supports converted traffic. Estimated 60-day build, then steady-state management.
  3. Layer in social-content production. Surgeon-on-camera Reels, before/after Reels, educational explainer video. Estimated 90-day initial build to a sustainable cadence.

Or any combination. The point is: the AEO foundation built in the first 90 days makes every downstream investment more efficient. Without it, paid ads and social are pouring water into a leaky funnel.

Why the timeline is 90 days and not 30

  • Schema indexing. Google typically takes 14 to 28 days to re-crawl, re-index, and surface newly schema-marked pages in AI Overviews. Schema deployed in week 3 is fully visible in AI answers no earlier than week 7.
  • Editorial-content authority. Newly published long-form articles need 30 to 60 days to settle in organic rank and become AI-citation eligible. Articles published in weeks 5 to 8 mature into citation-eligible status in weeks 9 to 14.
  • Review-velocity protection. RealSelf flags suspicious review surges. A push from 8 to 25 reviews must be paced across 8+ weeks to avoid auto-filter penalties.

A 30-day version of this work is technically possible. It would also be measurably worse than the 90-day version because the underlying algorithms reward consistency over time, not concentrated blasts.
