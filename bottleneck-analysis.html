Why this is happening

It is not a credentials problem.

You are board-certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery (ABPS), not a cosmetic-only board. You are SRQ Magazine Top Doctors 2024. You were featured in Sarasota Magazine in January 2025. You inherited the practice of Dr. James Marsh. You hold a 5.0 Google rating.

It is a machine-readability problem.

We audited your site for schema markup. The structured data that tells Google's AI "this surgeon is board-certified, here is their training, here are the procedures they offer, here are the answers to common patient questions" does not exist on your website.

Without that structured data, the AI cannot reliably surface you, no matter how much you have earned the position. Sessa, Hamwi, and HK win these citations partly because their sites carry the markup that lets Google's AI quote them as the answer.

The three bottlenecks, ranked by leverage

Bottleneck 01 · Zero schema markup anywhere on the site

We inspected the homepage, the About page, and the 6 priority procedure pages. None of them carry a single line of JSON-LD structured data. No MedicalBusiness, no Physician, no MedicalProcedure, no FAQPage, no BreadcrumbList, no AggregateRating. This is the direct AEO blocker.

Cost of doing nothing

Every week the schema does not exist is another week Sessa, Hamwi, and HK keep splitting the seven AI citations between themselves. The cosmetic surgery search volume in Sarasota does not pause.

Fix

MedicalBusiness + Physician + MedicalProcedure + FAQPage + BreadcrumbList + AggregateRating schema deployed across homepage + About + 6 priority procedure pages. 5 to 7 working days. Validates through Google's Rich Results Test before push.

Bottleneck 02 · No financing partners named above the fold

Your procedures price between $6,000 and $17,000+. The homepage does not name a single financing partner above the fold. The "Financing" link in the nav opens a generic page with no logos, no calculator, no decision tree.

Cost of doing nothing

Cosmetic surgery sites that surface partner logos plus a calculator above the form typically lift consult-form completion by 12 to 28%. Every consultation form abandoned because the patient scanned to find a price, saw "$8,500 to $12,000," and could not find a financing reassurance in the same scroll, is a consultation walked off the page.

Fix

Hero trust strip + financing partner row + procedure-page financing block + payment calculator. CareCredit, PatientFi, Cherry, Alphaeon Credit named explicitly above the fold. TILA-compliant footnote.

Bottleneck 03 · No editorial content, nothing for AI engines to cite

The procedure pages are well-built (each runs ~2,800 words, has FAQs, has before/after pairs, has a price range). That is the commercial layer. The educational layer, the long-form content Google's AI pulls from for queries like "what is a mommy makeover" or "how do I choose a plastic surgeon in Sarasota," does not exist on the site at all.

Cost of doing nothing

Every educational query a Sarasota patient runs while researching cosmetic surgery is currently being answered by a competitor's blog post or by Google's AI quoting a competitor's site.

Fix

4 long-form editorial articles (~2,000-2,200 words each), each AI-citation eligible, each internally linked to its commercial counterpart. Phase 2 of the 90-day plan.
