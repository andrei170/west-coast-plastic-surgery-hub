2. Website findings

  • Stack: Built by Influx Marketing (boutique plastic-surgery agency). Templated, clean, well-organised.
  • Layout: Full procedure tree in nav, hero CTA "Schedule Consultation," consult form (procedure dropdown + AM/PM preference) on every page.
  • Conversion path: Form + phone. No real online booking (no RevenueWell, NexHealth, or Zocdoc integration).
  • Financing partners listed: NONE BY NAME. Just a generic "Financing" link in nav. Direct conversion leak, $6K to $17K procedures need explicit financing reassurance above the fold.
  • Before/after gallery: Yes. Procedure pages have inline pairs (breast aug has 10+).
  • Schema / structured data: NONE. Verified zero JSON-LD on homepage and breast-aug page. No MedicalBusiness, Physician, FAQPage, Service, or Review schema. This is the AEO blocker.
  • Procedure pages: Each procedure has its own dedicated page, geo'd H1 ("Breast Augmentation in Sarasota, FL"), ~2,800 words, FAQ section, price range, before/after. Solid foundation, needs schema + citation formatting on top, not a rebuild.
  • URL pattern: /breast/breast-augmentation/ (not /breast-augmentation-sarasota/, minor SEO opportunity).
  • No blog. No editorial content. Nothing for AI engines to pull into educational-query answers.

Top 3 site bottlenecks

  1. Zero schema markup = invisible to AI overviews and Google Knowledge Panel reinforcement
  2. Financing partners not named = high-ticket conversion leak
  3. No blog / no editorial content = no citation surface for AI engines
